Breakfast for Dinner


Worked through the night

      Slept through the day again

            Wake up it's 5:00pm


Scavenge for a meal

      The fridge is empty

            Make something out of nothing




Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1¼ cups milk
  • 5 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • 8 slices of bacon

Directions

Yields dinner for two: 8 pancakes, 4 eggs, and 8 slices of bacon

  1. To make the pancakes, in a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, salt and sugar. Make a well in the center and pour in the milk, one egg, and melted butter; stir until combined. Leave some lumps in the batter and don't overmix as the pancakes will not rise.
  2. Set the batter aside, and start preparing your bacon.
  3. In a cold pan, arrange the bacon slices in a single layer. Don't let them overlap too much as they will not cook evenly. Heat the pan to medium heat and start cooking the bacon. Turn the pieces often to promote even browning. Keep flipping each slice until they reach your desired doneness (about 8-15 minutes.)
  4. Remove the bacon from the pan and leave it to drain on some paper towels.
  5. Pour out some of the bacon grease from the pan leaving just enough to lightly coat the bottom. This will be used to fry up the eggs.
  6. Turn the pan to medium heat and individually crack the eggs into the pan. Cover the eggs with a lid and leave them to cook until the white is firm but the yolk is still runny (about 3-4 minutes.)
  7. While you wait for the eggs to cook, start frying up the pancakes.
  8. Heat a lightly greased non-stick pan to medium-high heat. Pour or scoop the pancake batter onto the pan, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake. Cook for 2-3 minutes and then flip each pancake. Allow them to continue cooking for another 2-3 minutes or until golden-brown.
  9. Assemble the meal by plating the pancakes, bacon, and eggs. Serve with maple syrup, butter, salt, and pepper to taste.
